The beans

Crafting the perfect cup of coffee is a symphony of senses and technique. It begins with the careful selection of beans, each chosen for its unique characteristics and flavor profile. Roasting transforms these raw beans into aromatic gems, with temperature and duration tailored to bring out their nuanced flavors. Grinding the beans to the perfect consistency is an art in itself, ensuring an even extraction of flavor during brewing. Water, heated to precise temperatures, dances with the ground coffee, coaxing out its essence through a delicate infusion process.

Arabica

Arabica

Arabica beans are the most popular and widely consumed coffee beans worldwide. They are known for their smooth, mild flavor with hints of sweetness and acidity. Arabica beans typically have a lower caffeine content compared to other varieties.

Bourbon

Bourbon

Bourbon coffee beans are a variety of Arabica beans known for their rich, complex flavor profile. They often have a smooth, full-bodied taste with notes of caramel, chocolate, and fruit. Bourbon beans are cultivated in several countries, including Ethiopia, Rwanda, and Brazil.

Gesha

Gesha

Gesha (or Geisha):coffee beans are prized for their exceptional quality and unique flavor profile. They are often characterized by their floral and tea-like aroma, with delicate notes of jasmine, bergamot, and tropical fruits. Gesha beans are grown in limited quantities and are highly sought after by coffee enthusiasts.

Maragogipe

Maragogipe

Maragogipe beans, also known as "elephant beans" due to their large size, are a variety of Arabica beans known for their mild flavor and low acidity. They produce a smooth, creamy cup of coffee with subtle fruity and floral notes.

Robusta

Robusta

Robusta beans have a stronger, more bitter flavor profile compared to Arabica beans. They are often higher in caffeine and have a thicker crema when brewed, making them popular for use in espresso blends. Robusta beans are also more resilient to pests and diseases, making them easier to cultivate in different environments.

Typica

Typica

Typica is one of the oldest and most traditional coffee varieties. It is known for its balanced flavor profile, often featuring mild acidity and pleasant sweetness. Typica beans are grown in various regions around the world, including Central and South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia.

Cold Brew

Cool, Smooth, and Refreshing

Escape the heat with the cool and refreshing taste of cold brew. Slowly steeped over time, this method extracts the essence of coffee with a smooth and mellow finish. Sip on the subtle sweetness and low acidity that cold brew offers, as it invigorates your senses with every chilled sip. Perfect for hot summer days or any time you crave a refreshing pick-me-up, cold brew is a delightful twist on traditional brewing methods.

French Press

Bold and Uncompromising

Dive into the depths of boldness with the French press method. Embrace the robust flavors and velvety texture as the plunger extracts the essence of your coffee beans. With each plunge, you're greeted with a brew that's unapologetically rich and full-bodied. Experience the allure of French press brewing and awaken your taste buds to a symphony of intense flavors.

Siphon

The Art of Extraction

Step into the world of theatrical brewing with the siphon method. A mesmerizing display of science and artistry, the siphon brewer delivers a coffee experience like no other. Indulge in the sensory journey of siphon brewing and elevate your coffee ritual to a spectacle worth savoring.
